What I Found Out About My Instagram Ads!

When am I seeing Instagram ads the most?

Actually, looking at the times I saw Instagram ads, two patterns kind of like stood out.

The first was in the afternoon and evening, especially from around 3pm to 11pm. This makes sense because it’s usually when I usually take breaks and scroll through Instagram after doing work.

But, I also noticed there was a second peak around midnight and especially between 12am and 2am. At first, I felt that was surprising, but then I realised that I usually stay up late at night, either studying or just on my phone, so it fits with my routine.

Overall, this chart shows that the ads are showing up when I’m actually using Instagram the most.

Are these ads from big brands or independent sellers?

So, when I looked at who was sponsoring the Instagram ads I saw, the most common response was actually “Unsure”. For me, it was like I couldn’t easily tell who the ad was from and like either the account name didn’t stand out, or the post itself didn’t clearly show a brand. This shows that a lot of ads on Instagram aren’t always transparent enough about their sponsors.

Also, I noticed quite a lot of ads were from small businesses or independent sellers. This shows that Instagram is being used a lot by smaller brands, this is because it’s a good way for them to reach users like me without needing a big advertising budget.

Finally, the smallest group was from well-known brands like Nike or Apple. I expected to see more of these brands, but maybe I’m just not their main target audience right now or maybe the smaller brands are just more active in the spaces I scroll through.

Overall, I was just surprised that I couldn’t even identify most of the sponsors but it also shows how active small businesses are on Instagram.

Do these ads match my interests?

A boxplot showing how relevant I found the ads

I feel like most of the ads I saw was somewhat relevant. They kind of matched my interests, like things I’ve looked at before or hobbies I enjoy but they didn’t stand out enough to make me click or remember them.

There were a few number of ads were rated “Not relevant”, which shows that they felt random or unrelated to my interests. This also like shows that Instagram’s algorithm doesn’t always get it right — there are still ads that miss the mark.

Also, only a few ads felt “Very relevant”, like they really knew what I liked.

Therefore, this shows that most of the ads were in the middle, I feel like the ads were like a mix.
